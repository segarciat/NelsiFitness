So what does it take to reach your fitness goal? There are three things:

  • Commitment
  • Nutrition
  • Exercise

Commitment

How often do you stare at the mirror and tell yourself that something's got to change?

If you want to commit, you need to know what that means.

Goal

Do you want to be a runner? A lifter? Maybe you just want to gain or lose weight. Find what you want to seize, and let it steer you along.

Time

Managing your time efficiently is key so that you can work on yourself while maintaining a balanced life.

Money

Your goals will dictate what you need, and that may mean spending a little more. The upshot is that you are investing on something that matters to you; your body.

Nutrition

Your diet is the most essential part of your routine, and for many it is also the hardest part.

You must start by considering what macronutrients are, and how they relate to calories

Carbohydrates

  • Easily digested
  • Excellent for pre-workout source of energy
  • Oats, breads, fruits

Protein

  • Essential for muscle growth and repair
  • Source of essential amino acids
  • Broccoli, tofu, tree nuts, eggs, meats

Fats

  • Encourages natural hormone production and cell growth.
  • Needed to absorb fat-soluble vitamins, such as A, D, and E.
  • Raw nuts and seeds, tofu, cheeses, avacados.

Exercise

You grow strong muscles by exercising, not by watching others exercise.

Here are other things you should know:

Form over Weight

Focus on performing an exercise correctly to get all of its benefits and to minimize the likelihood of injury.

Incorporate variety

Balance out your routine by targetting different muscle groups; don't overwork the same muscle.

Rest sufficiently

Plan days off and rest between sets to let your muscles both grow and heal.
